About Me

I've always been a data collector, long before I knew what a data analyst was. One of my earliest memories is of watching anthills and carefully collecting information about the ants’ behavior. That curiosity about patterns and systems has followed me ever since. I’ve always found myself collecting information about the things around me and looking for relationships hidden within.

Professionally, I spent over six years working as a behaviorist, where collecting and analyzing behavioral data was a central part of my work. I bring that same analytical mindset to data analysis today, using tools like Python, SQL, and visualization platforms to explore datasets, clean and transform data, and surface meaningful insights. For me, working with data isn’t anything new, but a way of understanding the world that has been present for as long as I can remember.
